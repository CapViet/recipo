How to Make the Perfect Classic Caesar Salad at Home

Indulge in the timeless charm of a classic Caesar salad, with its tangy dressing, crunchy croutons, and tender romaine lettuce, all made from scratch at home.

Learn how to make the perfect classic Caesar salad and elevate your mealtime with a dish that's sure to impress. Follow our step-by-step guide to create an authentic Caesar salad that's both healthy and satisfying.

Introduction to the Classic Caesar Salad

A classic Caesar salad is a timeless staple of fine dining. Its crisp romaine lettuce, savory croutons, and rich, creamy dressing offer a harmonious balance of flavor and texture. The magic lies in the dressing — a key element that elevates this salad to a new level.

Ingredients

  • 1 large head of romaine lettuce, washed and torn
  • 1/2 cup croutons (homemade or store-bought)
  • 1/4 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup homemade Caesar dressing (recipe below)
  • 1/2 cup cooked chicken, steak, or shrimp (optional)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • Salt to taste

Homemade Caesar Dressing

  1. Prepare garlic and lemon: Mince 2 garlic cloves and squeeze 1 lemon.
  2. Combine ingredients: In a blender, combine garlic, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, pepper, and garlic powder.
  3. Emulsify: With the blender running, slowly pour in olive oil until thick and creamy.
  4. Add egg yolk and cheese: Blend in the egg yolk, then add Parmesan and blend until smooth.
  5. Season: Add salt to taste and adjust seasonings.
  6. Chill: Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Assembly Instructions

  1. Prep lettuce and croutons: Wash and dry romaine. Toast croutons at 350°F for 5–7 minutes.
  2. Assemble salad: In a bowl, combine lettuce, croutons, and Parmesan.
  3. Add dressing: Drizzle the chilled Caesar dressing and toss gently.
  4. Add protein (optional): Top with grilled chicken, steak, or shrimp.
  5. Garnish and serve: Sprinkle with parsley, extra cheese, and lemon wedges.

Protein Add-Ons

  • Grilled Chicken: Strips, wraps, or breast with croutons
  • Steak: Thinly sliced with Parmesan and lemon
  • Shrimp: Grilled with cheese and croutons
  • Others: Salmon, bacon, fried chicken strips, or tofu

Serving Tips

  • Use a large, sturdy bowl
  • Drizzle dressing at the table
  • Offer extra croutons and Parmesan
  • Garnish with parsley or lemon wedges
  • Serve as a main with soup or side salad

FAQs

Q: How long does it take to make a Caesar salad?

A: Prep time is 20–25 minutes. Assembly takes 10–15 minutes.

Q: Can you make a Caesar salad in advance?

A: Yes. Store croutons separately. Dress and assemble just before serving.

Q: Can I make a gluten-free Caesar salad?

A: Yes. Use gluten-free croutons and Worcestershire sauce.

Q: Can I make a vegan Caesar salad?

A: Yes. Replace Parmesan with nutritional yeast, use vegan Worcestershire, and add tofu or tempeh as protein.
