How to Make Juicy Grilled Hawaiian Chicken Skewers at Home

Get ready to transport your taste buds to the sun-kissed beaches of Hawaii with our easy recipe for juicy grilled Hawaiian chicken skewers. Marinated in a sweet and tangy mixture of pineapple juice, soy sauce, and ginger, these tender chicken kabobs are infused with the flavors of the islands.

Introduction

Grilled Hawaiian chicken skewers are a staple of summer cookouts and BBQs, and for good reason. These succulent, smoky bites of chicken are infused with the sweet and tangy flavors of pineapple, a classic combination that's hard to resist.

Ingredients List

  • Chicken breast: 1 lb, boneless and skinless, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • Pineapple juice: 1/2 cup
  • Coconut milk: 1/4 cup
  • Minced garlic: 2 cloves
  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Brown sugar: 2 tablespoons
  • Grated ginger: 1 tablespoon
  • Black pepper: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Salt: 1/2 teaspoon

Optional Add-ins

  • Red onion, sliced
  • Red bell pepper, sliced
  • Cherry tomatoes, halved
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Make the Marinade

In a blender or food processor, combine pineapple juice, coconut milk, garlic, soy sauce, brown sugar, ginger, pepper, and salt. Blend until smooth. Pour into a large bowl and stir to combine.

Step 2: Marinate the Chicken

Add the chicken to the marinade and mix well. Cover and refrigerate for 30 minutes to 2 hours.

Step 3: Thread onto Skewers

Thread the marinated chicken onto skewers, alternating with veggies if using. Leave a bit of space between each piece for even cooking.

Step 4: Grill the Skewers

Brush your grill with oil. Grill the skewers over medium-high heat for 10–12 minutes, turning occasionally, until chicken is cooked through and slightly charred.

Step 5: Serve and Garnish

Brush skewers with additional oil and pineapple juice for shine and flavor. Garnish with chopped cilantro, red onion, and cherry tomatoes as desired.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What's the best way to marinate the chicken?

A: Let the chicken sit in the marinade for at least 30 minutes, preferably 2 hours. You can refrigerate overnight or freeze for later use.

Q: Can I use wooden skewers?

A: Yes. Soak wooden skewers in water for 30 minutes before grilling to prevent burning.

Q: What type of grill works best?

A: Gas grills provide consistent heat and char marks, while charcoal grills add a smoky flavor.

Q: How do I keep the chicken juicy?

A: Don’t overcook. Grill until internal temp is 165°F (74°C), and brush with marinade during grilling for moisture.

Q: Can I prepare these skewers ahead of time?

A: Yes. You can marinate and assemble the skewers a day in advance. Keep them refrigerated until ready to grill.
