How to Make Panera-Style Monte Cristo Sandwiches at Home with a Sweet and Savory Twist

Bring the flavors of Panera Bread's iconic Monte Cristo sandwich into your home with our recipe. This sweet and savory twist on a classic sandwich is perfect for brunch or breakfast, with a crispy exterior and gooey filling that everyone will love.

What is a Monte Cristo Sandwich?

A Monte Cristo sandwich is a delightful mix of sweet and savory flavors. It usually includes ham, turkey, cheese, and jam, all grilled between two slices of bread. With a crispy exterior and melty interior, it's a brunch favorite for good reason.

Ingredients

  • Bread: 4 slices of day-old Challah or Brioche
  • Ham and Turkey: 2 slices each
  • Swiss Cheese: 2 slices
  • Jam: 2 tablespoons strawberry or raspberry jam
  • Butter: 2 tablespoons, softened

Optional Ingredients

  • Mayonnaise or Dijon Mustard: 1 tablespoon for extra flavor

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Bread

Slice bread into 1-inch thick pieces and toast or grill lightly for texture and flavor.

Step 2: Assemble the Sandwich

Spread mayo or mustard (optional), then layer ham, turkey, Swiss cheese, and jam. Top with the second slice of bread.

Step 3: Grill the Sandwich

Melt butter in a skillet over medium heat. Grill the sandwich for 2–3 minutes per side until golden brown and cheese is melted.

Step 4: Serve and Enjoy

Let the sandwich rest for 1–2 minutes. Slice in half and serve with fresh fruit or salad.

Dipping Sauces

  • Strawberry Jam: Classic sweet-and-tangy pairing
  • Cherry Jam: Fruity and rich
  • Chocolate Sauce: For an indulgent dessert-like bite
  • Maple Syrup: Adds a hint of earthiness and sweetness

Serving Suggestions

Pair with a fresh fruit salad, tomato soup, or crispy hash browns. For presentation, serve on a wooden board or tiered stand for a brunch-worthy display.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I use different cheeses?

A: Absolutely! Try cheddar, provolone, or mozzarella for different flavor profiles.

Q: What bread works best?

A: Challah, brioche, or any sturdy yet soft bread. Day-old bread holds up especially well.

Q: Can I prepare it ahead?

A: Assemble just before grilling for best results, but you can prep ingredients in advance.

Q: How do I get a crisp finish?

A: Butter both sides of the bread generously and cook over medium heat until golden brown and crisp.
