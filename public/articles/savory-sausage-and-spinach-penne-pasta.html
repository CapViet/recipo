How to Make Savory Sausage and Spinach Penne Pasta for a Quick and Hearty Meal

Savory sausage and spinach penne pasta is a comforting dish full of flavor and perfect for busy weeknights. With rich Italian sausage, fresh spinach, and a tangy tomato-broth sauce, this pasta is both hearty and satisfying. Whether you're cooking for your family or prepping meals for the week, this recipe is a keeper.

Introduction

There’s nothing quite like a warm bowl of pasta to brighten your day. This savory sausage and spinach penne pasta is flavorful, satisfying, and easy to make. With simple ingredients and quick preparation, it’s an ideal choice for a busy schedule or a comforting weekend dinner.

Ingredients List

  • Penne pasta: 1 lb
  • Sweet Italian sausage: 1 lb, casings removed
  • Fresh spinach: 1/2 cup, chopped
  • Garlic: 3 cloves, minced
  • Onion: 1 medium, diced
  • Chicken broth: 1 cup
  • Tomato paste: 2 tablespoons
  • Shredded Parmesan cheese: 1/2 cup (optional)
  • Salt: 1 tsp
  • Black pepper: 1/2 tsp
  • Olive oil: 1 tablespoon
  • Fresh basil leaves: 1/4 cup, chopped
  • Red pepper flakes: optional, to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Cook the Pasta

Boil a large pot of salted water and cook the penne pasta until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water, then drain and set aside.

Step 2: Sauté the Aromatics

In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add diced onion and minced garlic. Sauté for 3–4 minutes until the onion is translucent. Remove and set aside.

Step 3: Cook the Sausage

Add sausage to the skillet, breaking it into pieces as it browns. Cook until fully browned. Drain excess fat and set sausage aside.

Step 4: Cook the Spinach and Tomato Paste

Add tomato paste and spinach to the skillet. Sauté until spinach is wilted and tomato paste is fragrant (2–3 minutes). Return the sausage, onion, and garlic to the skillet.

Step 5: Simmer the Sauce

Pour in the chicken broth. Stir and bring to a simmer. Cook for 5–7 minutes until slightly thickened. Season with salt, pepper, and optional red pepper flakes.

Step 6: Combine Pasta and Sauce

Add cooked pasta to the sauce. Toss well to coat, using pasta water as needed for consistency. Stir in Parmesan if using. Adjust seasoning.

Step 7: Serve

Transfer to serving bowls or plates. Garnish with fresh basil. Serve hot and enjoy!

Serving Suggestions

  • Garlic Bread: Pair with garlic bread for a hearty Italian-style meal.
  • Green Salad: Serve with a crisp green salad for balance.
  • Roasted Vegetables: Complement with roasted zucchini or cherry tomatoes.

Flavor-Enhancing Tips

  • Use fresh ingredients: Fresh spinach and basil elevate the flavor.
  • Cook pasta al dente: Avoid overcooking to maintain the ideal texture.
  • Balance spice: Add red pepper flakes for heat, or omit for a milder dish.
  • Choose quality sausage: Good sausage makes a big difference in taste.
  • Finish with Parmesan: A generous sprinkle adds richness.

Serving Ideas

  • Individual Plates: Great for lunchboxes or meal prep.
  • Family Style: Serve from a large bowl at the center of the table.
  • Brunch Option: Try as a savory brunch dish with eggs on the side.

Frequently Asked Questions

What sausage is best?

A: Sweet Italian sausage is traditional, but spicy sausage works well if you like heat.

Can I use frozen spinach?

A: Yes, but thaw and squeeze out excess water before using.

How do I avoid sticky pasta?

A: Toss pasta with sauce immediately and reserve pasta water to loosen the mix.

Can this be made ahead?

A: Yes! Store in the fridge up to 3 days or freeze for up to 2 months.

What extras can I add?

A: Try adding cherry tomatoes, zucchini, or olives for variety.

Is it gluten-free?

A: Use gluten-free penne and ensure your broth and sausage are GF-friendly.
