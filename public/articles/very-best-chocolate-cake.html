How to Bake the Very Best Chocolate Cake from Scratch

Baking a chocolate cake from scratch is a labor of love—but it’s absolutely worth it. This guide will walk you through every step to achieve a moist, decadent cake with rich chocolate flavor that’s sure to impress.

Why This Recipe Works

With the right ingredients and techniques, you’ll get a perfect texture and deep chocolate flavor. Even novice bakers can master this cake by following the step-by-step instructions.

Key Ingredients

  • All-Purpose Flour: 2¾ cups for structure and tenderness.
  • Dutch-processed Cocoa Powder: 1 cup for deep chocolate flavor.
  • Granulated Sugar: 1¾ cups to sweeten and balance the bitterness.
  • Kosher Salt: ½ tsp to enhance all flavors.
  • Unsalted Butter: 1 cup, softened for richness and moisture.
  • Eggs: 4 large, at room temperature for structure and lift.
  • Buttermilk: 1 cup to add tanginess and tenderness.
  • Vanilla Extract: 1 tsp for aromatic depth.

Recommended Tools

  • Flour Sifter: For even texture.
  • Measuring Cups: Precision is key.
  • Baking Pan: 9-inch round or square pans work great.
  • Electric Mixer: To cream butter and sugar properly.
  • Whisk: For combining dry ingredients.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preheat the Oven

Set your oven to 350°F (175°C). A properly preheated oven ensures even baking.

Step 2: Prepare the Cake Pan

Grease and flour your cake pans or line with parchment paper to prevent sticking.

Step 3: Mix the Dry Ingredients

Whisk together flour, cocoa powder, sugar, salt, and baking powder in a bowl.

Step 4: Cream the Butter and Sugar

Use an electric mixer to beat butter until fluffy, then gradually add sugar. Beat until creamy (about 4 minutes).

Step 5: Beat in the Eggs

Add eggs one at a time, mixing well after each. This ensures a smooth, stable batter.

Step 6: Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

Alternately mix in dry ingredients and buttermilk to the butter mixture. Begin and end with the dry mix. Add vanilla last.

Step 7: Pour the Batter and Tap the Pan

Evenly divide the batter between pans. Tap lightly on the counter to remove air bubbles.

Step 8: Bake

Bake for 35–40 minutes. Test with a toothpick—it should come out clean. Cool in the pan 10 minutes, then transfer to wire racks.

Step 9: Frost

Once cooled, frost with your favorite chocolate buttercream or ganache. Decorate as desired with sprinkles, fruit, or shaved chocolate.

Tips for Success

  • Use Room Temp Ingredients: They mix more evenly and yield better texture.
  • Don’t Overmix: Stop once ingredients are just combined to avoid toughness.
  • Use Dutch-processed cocoa: For a smoother, more balanced flavor.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use natural cocoa powder instead?

A: Yes, but it may result in a more acidic flavor. Adjust baking soda accordingly.

How can I make this cake gluten-free?

A: Use a gluten-free flour blend and ensure other ingredients are certified GF.

Can I make cupcakes instead?

A: Absolutely. Reduce baking time to 18–22 minutes.

What’s the best frosting?

A: Chocolate buttercream or ganache pairs beautifully. Cream cheese frosting works if you prefer tangy contrast.
